Median Home Price to Median Household Income Ratio

Median Home Price to Median Household Income Ratio from 1984 to 2025

Published: December 9, 2025

Tags: Coding, Real Estate, Economics

By Luke Buck

As a young professional, the significant rise in home prices since the pandemic has been discouraging. I brought this up in conversation with some older friends recently and we began talking about median home prices compared to median household income. One older friend recalled buying a home in the 80s for roughly three times his household income. Today though, young homebuyers are lucky if they can purchase a home for five times their annual household income. See the history of this ratio below.

Though young people searching for homes today may feel overwhlelmed, we can see that the housing market has cooled since its 2022 high, when median priced homes were selling for nearly six times the median household income. More recently, the ratio has dipped below five, a level which has been reached several times since 2000. Still, the ratio has trended upward since 2000, and certainly has not returned to the levels seen in the 80s and 90s.

I gathered this data from Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED). Median home prices were availible quarterly, however median household income was only available annually. Hope you enjoy!
